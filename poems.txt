The sun was bright on pixel plains,
As I laid my blocks and dodged the rains.
A castle rose, a work of art,
A cozy home, a brand new start.

With every block, a happy sigh,
My blocky masterpiece reached for the sky.
But shadows creep when you're alone,
A silent figure, heart of stone.

They came by night, with TNT,
A wicked laugh, for all to see.
The walls came down, the glass did break,
A trail of ruin in their wake.

My chests are looted, treasures gone,
A lonely crater greets the dawn.
A monument to all my pain,
Washed away by pixel rain.

But in the dust, a single block,
A seed of hope, against the clock.
I'll build again, I will not yield,
Upon this scarred and broken field. For in this world of block and stone, the truest strength is in the will to rebuild and carry on.
